Nevada Semipalatinsk

Anti-nuclear movement in Kazakhstan

Few facts about movement:

  • The anti-nuclear movement in Kazakhstan, "Nevada Semipalatinsk", was formed in 1989 and was one of the first major anti-nuclear movements in the former Soviet Union.
  • It was led by famous kazakh writer Olzhas Suleimenov and attracted thousands of people to its protests and campaigns which eventually led to the closure of the nuclear test site in Kazakhstan in 1991.
  • The movement was named "Nevada Semipalatinsk" in order to show solidarity with similar movements in the United States aiming to close the Nevada Test Site.
  • The Soviet Union conducted 456 nuclear weapons tests at the Semipalatinsk Test Site, between 1949 and 1989.
  • The Semipalatinsk Test Site took the territory of the 18,000 km2, and comprised an area the size of Wales.
  • More than one million people around Semipalatinsk were exposed to radiation, which caused birth defects and cancer.
  • Nevada-Semipalatinsk played a positive role in promoting public understanding of "the necessity to fight against nuclear threats". The movement gained global support and, became "a real historical factor in finding solutions to global ecological problems".
